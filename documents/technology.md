# 10 Cool Technology Facts

### 1. The First Computer Mouse Was Made of Wood
Doug Engelbart invented the first computer mouse in 1964, and its casing was carved from wood.

### 2. A Modern Smartphone is Millions of Times More Powerful Than Apollo 11's Computer
The guidance computer that took Apollo 11 to the moon had about 64KB of memory. A typical modern smartphone has over 500,000 times more RAM and is millions of times faster.

### 3. The "Cloud" is Actually Underwater
Major internet companies run massive data centers on the ocean floor for better cooling and energy efficiency, with hundreds of thousands of servers in pressurized, water-cooled pods.

### 4. QR Codes Were Invented for the Auto Industry
Created in 1994 by a subsidiary of Toyota, QR (Quick Response) codes were designed to track vehicles and parts during manufacturing with high-speed component scanning.

### 5. The First 1GB Hard Drive Weighed Over 500 Pounds
In 1980, IBM released the first 1GB hard drive. It was the size of a refrigerator, weighed about 550 pounds (250 kg), and cost $40,000.

### 6. CAPTCHA Tests Help Digitize Books
The reCAPTCHA system uses words that optical character recognition (OCR) software can't read from old books and newspapers, using human input to help digitize them.

### 7. There's Enough Bitcoin to Give Everyone on Earth a Fraction
The total number of Bitcoin that will ever exist is capped at 21 million. With a global population of over 8 billion, there will never be a whole Bitcoin for every person.

### 8. The First Webcam Was Invented to Monitor a Coffee Pot
In 1991, researchers at Cambridge University set up a camera to monitor their shared coffee pot, saving them a wasted trip to an empty pot. This was one of the first webcams.

### 9. A Single Google Search Uses More Computing Power Than the Entire Apollo Program
The complex algorithms and data centers that process a single Google query represent far more computational power than what was available for the entire Apollo moon mission.

### 10. Wi-Fi Was an Accidental Discovery
While experimenting with cellphone antenna technology in 1992, Australian astronomer John O'Sullivan discovered a way to transmit data without wires, leading to the development of Wi-Fi.
